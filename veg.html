Paneer Butter Masala(India)

A North Indian classic, Paneer Butter Masala is famous for its creamy, mildly spiced tomato gravy. It’s a restaurant favorite and a comfort food for many vegetarians.

Paneer Butter Masala is a beloved North Indian dish that originated in the rich culinary traditions of Punjab. Known for its creamy, buttery tomato gravy and soft paneer cubes, it became a restaurant favorite across India in the late 20th century. Its popularity quickly spread worldwide through Indian restaurants, making it one of the most ordered vegetarian curries. There are many ways to prepare it — from the classic restaurant-style with cream and butter to lighter home versions using yogurt or cashew paste. Loved by millions, its balance of mild spices and indulgent texture has earned it a permanent place in the hearts (and plates) of food lovers everywhere.

What You’ll Need

  • Paneer(cottage cheese)
  • Butter
  • Onions
  • Tomatoes
  • Ginger
  • Cashew nuts
  • Fresh cream
  • Garam masala
  • Kasuri methi(dried fenugreek leaves)

Your Path to Deliciousness

  1. Sauté onions, tomatoes, and cashews until soft.
  2. Blend into a smooth paste.
  3. Heat butter, add paste and spices.
  4. Stir in cream and kasuri methi.
  5. Add paneer cubes and simmer briefly.

Margherita Pizza(Italy)

This pizza is a symbol of Italian pride, created to represent the colors of the Italian flag. Its simplicity and freshness make it a global favorite.

Margherita Pizza, born in Naples in the late 19th century, was created to honor Queen Margherita with the colors of the Italian flag—red tomatoes, white mozzarella, and green basil. Over time, it became a symbol of authentic Italian pizza, loved worldwide for its simplicity and fresh flavors. From wood-fired Neapolitan to thin-crust variations, there are many ways to enjoy this classic, and its timeless charm continues to win hearts across the globe.

What You’ll Need

  • Pizza dough (made from flour, water, yeast, salt, and olive oil)
  • Fresh mozzarella cheese
  • Fresh tomatoes (or tomato sauce)
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Extra virgin olive oil
  • Salt

Your Path to Deliciousness

  1. Preheat oven to 250 °C (480 °F) and place pizza stone/tray inside.
  2. Roll out pizza dough into a thin round base.
  3. Spread a thin layer of tomato sauce evenly over the base.
  4. Add fresh mozzarella slices on top.
  5. Sprinkle fresh basil leaves and drizzle olive oil.
  6. Bake for 7–10 minutes until crust is golden and cheese is bubbling.

Falafel (Middle East)

Falafel is a beloved street food, known for its crispy exterior and soft, herby inside. It’s a vegan protein powerhouse enjoyed in wraps and salads.

Falafel is a popular Middle Eastern street food made from ground chickpeas, herbs, and spices, shaped into balls or patties, and fried until golden. It’s loved worldwide for its crispy outside and soft, flavorful inside, often served with pita bread, fresh salad, and tahini sauce.

What You’ll Need

  • Dried chickpeas (soaked overnight)
  • Onion
  • Garlic
  • Fresh parsley & cilantro
  • Ground cumin & coriander
  • Salt, pepper, baking soda
  • Oil for frying

Your Path to Deliciousness

  1. Soak dried chickpeas overnight and drain well.
  2. Blend chickpeas, onion, garlic, herbs, and spices into a coarse paste.
  3. Mix in baking soda and rest for 30 minutes.
  4. Shape mixture into small balls or patties.
  5. Deep fry until golden brown and crispy.
  6. Serve hot with pita bread, salad, and tahini sauce.

Vegetable Biryani (India)

Vegetable Biryani is a festive Indian rice dish, layered with spices and vegetables. It’s a celebration food with royal roots and endless regional variations.

Vegetable Biryani is a fragrant and colorful rice dish from India, layered with aromatic basmati rice, fresh vegetables, and a blend of exotic spices. Originating in royal Mughal kitchens, it became popular across India for its rich taste and festive appeal, with countless regional variations loved by vegetarians and non-vegetarians alike.

What You’ll Need

  • Basmati rice
  • Mixed vegetables (carrot, beans, peas, potatoes)
  • Onions
  • Tomatoes
  • Ginger-garlic paste
  • Biryani spices (cumin, cinnamon, cloves, cardamom)
  • Yogurt
  • Fresh herbs (mint, coriander)
  • Saffron soaked in milk
  • Ghee or oil

Your Path to Deliciousness

  1. Rinse and soak basmati rice, then par-cook until 70% done.
  2. Sauté onions, tomatoes, and ginger-garlic paste in ghee.
  3. Add vegetables, biryani spices, and yogurt; cook until tender.
  4. Layer rice over the vegetable mixture with mint, coriander, and saffron milk.
  5. Cover and cook on low heat (dum) until flavors blend and rice is fluffy.
  6. Serve hot with raita or salad.

Ratatouille (France)

Ratatouille is a rustic French stew, made famous by the animated film. It’s a celebration of summer vegetables and Provençal flavors.

Ratatouille is a classic French Provençal dish made with fresh vegetables like eggplant, zucchini, bell peppers, onions, and tomatoes, slow-cooked with herbs for a rich, hearty flavor. Originating in Nice, it was once considered humble peasant food but has gained global fame, especially after the 2007 animated film, and is now celebrated for its rustic charm and versatility.

What You’ll Need

  • Eggplant
  • Zucchini
  • Bell peppers
  • Tomatoes
  • Onions
  • Garlic
  • Olive oil
  • Fresh herbs (thyme, basil, parsley)
  • Salt & pepper

Your Path to Deliciousness

  1. Chop all vegetables into evenly sized pieces.
  2. Sauté onions and garlic in olive oil until soft.
  3. Add eggplant, zucchini, and bell peppers; cook until slightly tender.
  4. Stir in tomatoes, herbs, salt, and pepper.
  5. Simmer on low heat until vegetables are tender and flavors meld.
  6. Serve hot as a side or main dish with bread or rice.
